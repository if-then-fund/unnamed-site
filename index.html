We found a loophole to get DC a vote in Congress.

Why? The District doesn’t have a voice in Congress.

The District of Columbia sends a non-voting delegate to Congress. Even though the District has a larger population than some states, residents have no voice in Congress.

That’s not right. We want a representative who can vote.

We’re adopting Pennsylvania’s 8th congressional district.

We’re going to pour money into Pennsylvania’s 8th congressional district election. We’ll back the Democratic candidate with an avalanche of support, making DC a very special interest.

If our candidate wins, we’ll have a voice in Congress through a representative who can actually vote. Join us by using the form to the rightbelow to make a contribution to our candidate.

It’s that simple. We back a candidate in another district so we get a voice in Congress.

Why Pennsylvania’s 8th?

We chose a district where we have a real chance to shape the outcome of an election. The current congressman isn’t seeking reelection, so the seat will be open and is considered a toss-up.

The district is located just northeast of Philadelphia.

We are DC residents who want to make a difference.

This is a project of Joshua Tauberer, a civic hacker and expert in government transparency and accountability, and Jonathan Zucker, a campaign finance attorney and political technology entrepreneur. Tauberer and Zucker are also co-founders of if.then.fund. Both live in the District.
